This epic point-to-point demands a car shuttle but rewards with one of the most dramatic descents on the mountain: starting at Aspen Basin (10,300ft), you climb past the radio towers to Tesuque Peak (12,040ft) through exposed, technical terrain before dropping nearly 5,000 feet over nine miles all the way down to Tesuque village (7,200ft). The upper section stays buried in snow until June, but once clear, the ridge near the towers offers big views alongside a serious quad-burning commitment. The lower canyon is the payoff — shaded singletrack along Tesuque Creek lined with wildflowers in July, cool and almost otherworldly compared to the punishment above. Your legs will hate you for days.
