This is the classic Santa Fe climb — the one basically every local has done at least once and most have done dozens of times. The steep, rocky grind up to the 9121ft summit will have your lungs working hard, but you're rewarded with sweeping views of the city spread out below and the Jemez Mountains rolling away to the west. The trailhead sits right behind St. John's College, which means zero excuses about the drive.

Summer heat on this south-facing climb can be brutal — spring and fall are where this route really shines. Weekends in good weather draw a crowd, so go early or plan on some company on the trail.
