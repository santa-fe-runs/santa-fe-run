A great early-season option when higher trails are still buried — this 6-mile loop starts at the lower Hyde Park trailhead and sends you straight up paved Hyde Park Road for a couple miles of honest climbing to the ski resort. The road is runnable all the way up, which is great news going out and terrible news for your quads on the way back down. Return on beautiful singletrack through shaded ponderosa forest, the kind of running that makes you forget how much the descent already took from you. Popular with hikers and runners alike on weekends, so don't expect solitude — but do expect 1,400 feet of gain and legs that know they worked.
