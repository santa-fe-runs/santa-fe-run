The name says it all. This brutal point-to-point drops 4,800 feet over 9 miles — from Aspen Basin down through the radio towers on Tesuque Peak all the way to Tesuque village — and it will absolutely destroy your quads. You'll need a car shuttle, which means you have to commit, no bailing early. The upper section near the towers is exposed and technical, the kind of terrain where you're picking your feet up and paying attention; in a good year the snow doesn't clear until June, so plan accordingly. But then the canyon opens up and the lower stretch through Tesuque Creek is something else entirely — cool shade, wildflowers in July, the creek doing its thing — and suddenly you remember why you signed up for this. You'll arrive in the village with aching legs and a face that can't quite decide whether to grin or grimace. Worth it. Probably.
