Atalaya Mountain is the classic Santa Fe sufferfest — a steep, rocky grind to a 9,121-foot summit that just about every local has done at least once. The trail starts right in the city behind St. John's College and climbs roughly 1,900 feet over about 6 miles out and back, rewarding the effort with sweeping views of Santa Fe below and the Jemez Mountains rolling out to the west. It's a popular one, so expect company on weekends and in good weather. Summer heat can be brutal on the exposed upper slopes, making spring and fall the far better seasons to tackle this one.
