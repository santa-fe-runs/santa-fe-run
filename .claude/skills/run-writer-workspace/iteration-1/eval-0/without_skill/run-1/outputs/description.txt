Starting from the Hyde Park trailhead at the base of the mountain, this route climbs steeply on paved Hyde Park Road for roughly two miles before meeting the trail near the ski resort — a grind that's surprisingly runnable on the way up but will leave your quads begging on the descent. The return leg rewards the effort with shaded singletrack winding through gorgeous ponderosa pine forest. At around 6 miles and 1,400 feet of gain, it's a solid blue-rated workout that draws a busy weekend crowd of both hikers and runners. Because it starts low, it's also one of the best options when higher terrain is still buried in snow.
