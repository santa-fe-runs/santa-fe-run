The Winsor Trail to Lake Katherine is one of the premier mountain running routes in Northern New Mexico — a demanding out-and-back that climbs from the base of Ski Santa Fe at 10,500 feet to a pristine alpine lake tucked beneath the 12,622-foot summit of Santa Fe Baldy. It's the kind of run that earns its views.

The trail begins at the ski area parking lot and immediately enters the Pecos Wilderness, climbing through dense spruce-fir forest before breaking into open tundra above treeline. The final two miles to Lake Katherine are above 12,000 feet and fully exposed — the kind of terrain where the sky feels enormous and the wind can cut hard even on a warm afternoon.

**The Route**

The Winsor Trail (#254) is the primary artery of the Santa Fe Wilderness. From the trailhead, the route climbs steadily through old-growth conifer forest, crosses several small streams in the first few miles (reliable water through mid-summer), and gains the ridge near Penitente Peak before the final descent to Lake Katherine at mile 7.1.

The lake sits in a glacially-carved cirque ringed by talus and tundra. On calm days it mirrors the peaks above. On most days, the wind has other plans.

**Conditions and Timing**

This trail is snow-covered into late June most years and should only be attempted by confident off-trail navigators before July. The prime window is July through mid-September. Afternoon thunderstorms are a daily occurrence in July and August — plan your turnaround to be off the exposed ridge above treeline by noon.

**Tips**

- This is real wilderness running. Carry navigation (the CalTopo map linked above), layers, rain gear, and emergency supplies.
- Altitude: The trailhead starts at 10,500 feet and the route climbs to 12,622 feet. Visitors from lower elevations should acclimatize for a day or two before attempting this run.
- Water is available from streams in the lower canyon through mid-summer; always treat or filter.
- No fee required; Pecos Wilderness regulations apply. Leave No Trace.
