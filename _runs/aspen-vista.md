The Aspen Vista Trail is the quintessential Santa Fe fall run — a gentle out-and-back through one of the highest aspen groves in the Sangre de Cristo Mountains. Starting near 10,000 feet along Hyde Park Road (the road to Ski Santa Fe), the trail winds through a cathedral of quaking aspens before opening onto sweeping views of the Jemez Mountains and, on clear days, all the way to Mount Taylor.

The trail gains elevation gradually, making it accessible for runners who are new to high-altitude running. The footing is generally good — compact dirt with some exposed roots in the aspens — though early summer can bring muddy sections as the snowpack melts.

**Fall Colors**

The prime window for peak fall color is typically the last two weeks of September into early October, though this shifts by a week or two from year to year depending on temperature. Catch it at the right moment and the hillsides turn a luminous gold against an impossibly blue sky — one of the great natural spectacles in New Mexico.

**Tips**

- Start early in summer to avoid afternoon thunderstorms, which can move in quickly above 10,000 feet.
- Bring layers: temperatures at the trailhead are usually 10–15°F cooler than downtown Santa Fe.
- Dogs are welcome on leash.
- The trail is part of the Santa Fe National Forest; no permit is required.
