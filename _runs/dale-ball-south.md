The Dale Ball Trails are Santa Fe's backyard trail system — a network of nearly 22 miles of singletrack winding through piñon and juniper foothills just minutes from downtown. The Southern Section is the most technical and arguably the most scenic, threading between rocky ridgelines with views back toward the Sangre de Cristos and out toward the Jemez.

This 6.8-mile loop links several named trails — La Tierra, Cerro Gordo, and the Southern Dale Ball connector — to create a satisfying figure-eight that samples the best terrain the system has to offer. Expect loose sandy soil in the flats, fun rocky technical sections on the ridgelines, and a few short punchy climbs.

**Conditions**

The Dale Ball Trails run year-round but shine in spring and fall when temperatures are moderate and the desert flora is at its most vivid. Summer running is possible but demanding — afternoon temperatures in the piñon-juniper zone regularly hit the 90s, and there's little shade. Winters are surprisingly runnable; light snowfall tends to melt within a day or two at these elevations.

**Trail Character**

Unlike the high-mountain trails above Santa Fe, the Dale Ball system stays in the 7,200–7,850-foot range — high enough to feel removed from town, low enough to be comfortably runnable for visitors still adjusting to altitude.

**Tips**

- Trail intersections are well-signed; download a map before heading out.
- Popular with mountain bikers — stay alert on fast descents.
- No water sources on trail; carry everything you need.
- Dogs welcome off-leash in some sections; check current regulations.
