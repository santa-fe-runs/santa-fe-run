The Borrego – Bear's Wallow Loop is the quintessential introductory trail run in the Santa Fe foothills — a 4-mile circuit through pine, fir, and aspen forest that follows three historic trails along Tesuque Creek. It starts lower and easier than the high-country routes above Ski Santa Fe, making it a great option when snow still lingers above 9,000 feet or when you want a shorter, shadier run close to town.

The loop stitches together three named trails: Borrego (#150), a historic sheep-drive route used to move flocks between northern villages and the Santa Fe market; Winsor (#254), a designated National Recreation Trail that runs along Big Tesuque Creek; and Bear Wallow (#182), which pulls steeply uphill through a shaded drainage to close the loop.

**The Route**

From the trailhead, the Borrego Trail descends gently through a mixed conifer forest into a shaded valley. At the half-mile junction, bear right to stay on Borrego #150, which climbs through a series of switchbacks to a broad ridge before dropping back down to Big Tesuque Creek. A fallen log serves as a footbridge across the creek. The route then joins the Winsor Trail (#254) heading westward for roughly a mile along the creek — flat, fast, and pleasant running through aspens and willows, with two creek crossings that can be wet in spring.

At the next signed junction, turn left onto Bear Wallow Trail (#182). This is the only sustained climb of the loop, ascending steeply through the wallow drainage back up to the ridge. The trail signs are reliable throughout; you are unlikely to lose the route.

**Conditions and Timing**

At 8,200 feet, this trail comes into shape earlier in the season than the high-country routes. Snow generally clears by early May, and the creek crossings — while cold — are manageable through late spring. Summers are lush: wildflowers are dense in June and July, and the aspen show colors in late September. The trail stays runnable into November most years.

**Tips**

- This is one of the busiest trailheads in the Santa Fe National Forest. Expect company on weekends.
- Dogs are welcome. The trail is shared with mountain bikers — especially on Borrego and Bear Wallow.
- The creek crossings on the Winsor section can be knee-deep during peak snowmelt (April–May); trail runners in light shoes should expect wet feet.
- Afternoon thunderstorms are common July through August. The loop is below treeline, but plan accordingly.
- Cell service is spotty past the parking lot. Download the CalTopo map offline before heading out.
